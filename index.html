Personal details

I’m Jed Dale, I’m currently a PhD student in the Department of Earth & Planetary Sciecne at Washington University in Saint Louis. I work with PI Dr. Claire Masteller in the WUSTL Geomorphology Lab. Ive previously worked with the Beach-Butzer Soils, Sediemnts & Geoarchaeolgy Lab at the University of Texas at Austin (PIs: Dr. Timothy Beach & Dr. Sheryl Luzzadder-Beach), and with Dr. Clark Erickson & Dr. Douglas Jerolmack at the University of Pennsylvania.

–

I’m interested in studying physical and biological controls on sediment transport and landscape evolution with a combination of experiments, remote sensing, and modeling (all rooted in fieldwork). I’m fascinated by the ways in which life, from the scale of bacteria to forests, can shape the form and function of landscapes, with an emphasis on rivers. I also have an ongoing interest in geoarchaeology and long-term human interactions with landscape, especially in the Pre-Columbian neotropics.

–

Current Projects Include:

* Large wood and wood salvage in tropical rivers
* Feedbacks between cohesion and river form
* Tufa streams and geochemical influence on channel morphology
